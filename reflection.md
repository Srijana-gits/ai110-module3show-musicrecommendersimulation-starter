# Reflection — Profile Comparisons

---

## High-Energy Pop vs. Chill Lofi
Complete opposites, not a single song overlapped. High energy pulled fast danceable tracks; low energy pulled slow instrumentals. This is the system working exactly as intended.

---

## High-Energy Pop vs. Deep Intense Rock
Both want high energy, so "Gym Hero" shows up for both. Here's why: Gym Hero is pop/intense with energy 0.93. The Pop profile gets it for genre match; the Rock profile gets it for mood match ("intense"). It's like asking a friend for happy pop and getting a gym anthem — it checks most boxes even if the vibe is slightly off. There simply aren't enough happy pop songs in the catalog to fill five spots.

---

## Chill Lofi vs. Deep Intense Rock
Zero songs in common. Lofi got quiet background tracks; Rock got "Storm Runner" and "Iron Cathedral." Energy and mood are so far apart for these two profiles that the system correctly separated them completely.

---

## High-Energy Pop vs. Adversarial (High-Energy + Sad Mood)
Both want high energy pop, but the sad mood pulled "3AM Heartbreak" into the adversarial top 5. The problem: no song in the catalog is both high energy AND sad, so the system split the playlist between two completely different vibes, upbeat pop and a slow sad ballad mixed together.

---

## Adversarial (Angry Mood + Lofi Genre) vs. Chill Lofi
Both ask for lofi but "Iron Cathedral" (metal/angry) ranked #1 for the angry profile because mood wins over genre. The system picked a side, it chose mood first which is probably wrong here since most lofi listeners are choosing a vibe and context, not just a raw emotion.
